About

I am a Post-Doctoral Researcher specializing in Computer Vision and Medical Image Analysis. My research focuses on the intersection of Generative AI (LLMs) and Radiological Workflows, specifically targeting the automation of lumbar spine MRI interpretation.

My current work addresses the computational bottlenecks of deploying Large Multimodal Models (LMMs) in clinical settings. I am actively researching Knowledge Distillation strategies to compress large foundation models (e.g., MedGemma, BioMedCLIP) into efficient, edge-deployable architectures without compromising clinical accuracy.

Featured Project

Teacher (MedGemma) -> Student (ResNet/ViT)

Knowledge Distillation from MedGemma

Compressing Large Multimodal Models for rapid lumbar spine reporting.

Methodology

We utilize a teacher model \( T \) (MedGemma-2b) to guide a lightweight student model \( S \) (Swin-UNETR Hybrid). The training objective minimizes the divergence between the teacher's report embeddings and the student's output, formulated as:

$$ \mathcal{L}_{total} = \alpha \mathcal{L}_{CE} + (1-\alpha) \tau^2 \mathcal{L}_{KL}\left(\sigma\left(\frac{z_s}{\tau}\right), \sigma\left(\frac{z_t}{\tau}\right)\right) $$

Where \( \mathcal{L}_{CE} \) is the standard Cross-Entropy loss on ground-truth reports, \( \mathcal{L}_{KL} \) is the Kullback-Leibler divergence, and \( \tau \) is the temperature parameter.
